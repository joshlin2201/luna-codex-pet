# Luna Reference Analysis

This run uses the earlier Luna photos plus the newest five reference images.

## Accuracy Targets

- Luna's most recognizable pet shape is a long, low side or three-quarter loaf.
- The body should read like a soft horizontal oval/cylinder, not a round upright bunny mascot.
- The white chest, cheek fluff, dewlap, and subtle lionhead ruff form a large soft front mass that wraps over the tucked front-leg area.
- Her head sits low into that dewlap/chest mass. The chin and lower muzzle look cushioned by the dewlap rather than perched above it.
- In side loafs, the low white dewlap drops close to the floor line and hides most of the front feet.
- Front feet are usually hidden. When visible, they are small rabbit feet tucked under the chest, pointing downward or slightly outward.
- Hindquarters are a large mottled gray saddle/oval with small tail or heel cues only when the pose needs them.
- Her hind legs are long. When tucked into loaf position, they create a steep raised gray haunch/rump behind the low front dewlap, making the loaf shorter and more compact front-to-back.
- When the hind legs are stretched out, they hang or trail to the side/behind the body as long rabbit feet, making the body read much flatter and longer.
- Movement and jumps should exaggerate her real long stretched shape, not create cartoon running legs.
- Running/hopping must show a load-to-kick cycle: forefeet reach/land first, haunch compresses, hind legs extend and kick backward through toe-off, body flattens/elongates, then hind feet recover under the body.
- Rabbit hind feet are long functional levers, not little rounded paws. They should change angle during stance and toe-off rather than staying as static nubs.
- Restore coat detail from earlier Luna passes: mottled gray saddle, irregular gray side patches, dark mask edges, and subtle side speckling. Avoid simplified zebra bands or smooth generic gray.

## Signature Markings

- White blaze down the face into a white nose, muzzle, and chest.
- Dark gray-brown eye mask on both sides; stronger viewer-left dark patch in front-facing poses.
- Quirky slightly squinty left eye under the mask.
- Mottled gray saddle and side patches over a white body.
- Upright, slightly asymmetric ears with dark outside edges and pale inner ear.
- Wispy lionhead-mix side tufts around cheeks, jaw, neck, and behind the ears.

## State Guidance

- `idle`, `waving`, `failed`, `waiting`, `running`, and `review`: default to low loaf anatomy with legs tucked underneath.
- In loaf states, keep Luna's head low on the dewlap: no tall neck, no upright chest column, and no separated head perched above the body.
- `waving`: greeting is an ear flick, tiny head tilt, blink, and nose twitch. Never use a raised foreleg wave.
- `running`: task-processing is focused loaf motion, not literal running.
- `review`: use the quirky left-eye squint, head tilt, and ear angle. Avoid props and paw/hand gestures.
- `running-right`, `running-left`, and `jumping`: show a real rabbit stretch-compress body. Feet must appear where motion requires them: forefeet reach/land, hind feet kick backward and recover.
- Tucked hind-leg frames should be shorter and steeper at the rump. Extended hind-leg frames should be flatter/longer with long trailing rabbit feet to the side/behind.

## Reject Criteria

- Generic round bunny body.
- Visible paw pads, dog/cat feet, hand shapes, fingers, or arm-like forelegs.
- Front feet projecting like arms from the chest.
- Short generic back paws instead of long tucked or trailing rabbit hind feet.
- Running rows where the feet do not visibly extend/kick through the stride.
- A half-state rump that is neither steep/short tucked loaf nor flat/long extended body.
- Oversimplified coat pattern that loses Luna's mottled gray saddle and side patch detail.
- Humanized waving or typing.
- Detached effects, props, scenery, shadows, guide marks, or readable text.
