# Luna Identity Guide

Use this when repairing or regenerating Luna.

Luna is not a generic round bunny mascot. Her default resting shape is a compact, low side or three-quarter rabbit loaf. Her head sits low into the dewlap: the chin and lower muzzle rest on the soft white chest cushion rather than perching above a tall neck. In normal loaf states, all legs are tucked underneath. The folded long hind legs make the rear shorter and steeper, with a raised gray haunch behind the low front. The fluffy dewlap and subtle lionhead cheek/neck ruff wrap over the front legs, so feet are hidden or only tiny toes barely peek downward under the chest.

Signature traits:

- Very long body, especially during jumps and directional movement.
- Low head-on-dewlap silhouette, with no tall neck.
- Steep raised gray haunch when the long hind legs are tucked.
- Long kicking/trailing rabbit hind feet when the back legs extend.
- Mottled gray saddle and irregular side patches rather than smooth gray or simple stripes.
- White blaze down the face, white muzzle, and white chest.
- Dark gray-brown eye mask, with the stronger dark patch on viewer-left when front-facing.
- Quirky slightly squinty left eye under the dark mask.
- Upright expressive ears that may be slightly asymmetric.
- Subtle lionhead-mix ruff around cheeks, jaw, neck, and side tufts.
- Gray saddle and side patches over a white body.
- Rabbit feet only when motion requires them: no paw pads, hands, arm-like shapes, or dog/cat feet.

State guidance:

- Resting states should read as tucked loaf first, expression second.
- Tucked loaf states should keep the head low on the dewlap and show a steep rear haunch from folded long hind legs.
- Greeting should use ear flick/head tilt, not lifted foreleg waving.
- Jumping and directional movement should use rabbit gait anatomy: forefeet reach/land, hindquarters coil, long hind feet kick through toe-off, body flattens/extends, then feet recover. Do not use cartoon running legs.
- Failed, waiting, running, and review should be expressed through ears, eyes, nose, head, and ruff motion without props.
